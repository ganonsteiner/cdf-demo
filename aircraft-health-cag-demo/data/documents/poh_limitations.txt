CESSNA MODEL 172N SKYHAWK
PILOT'S OPERATING HANDBOOK — SECTION 2: LIMITATIONS
1978 Edition (FAA Approved)

Type: Cessna 172N (1978) · Engine: Lycoming O-320-H2AD

================================================================================
AIRSPEED LIMITATIONS
================================================================================

Vne — Never Exceed Speed:                163 KIAS
  Do not exceed this speed in any operation.

Vno — Maximum Structural Cruising Speed: 128 KIAS
  Do not exceed this speed except in smooth air and then only with caution.

Va — Maneuvering Speed:                   99 KIAS (at 2300 lbs gross weight)
  Do not make full or abrupt control inputs above this speed.
  Decrease Va with decreasing gross weight.

Vfe — Maximum Flap Extended Speed:        85 KIAS
  Do not exceed this speed with flaps extended.

Vlo — Maximum Landing Gear Speed:         N/A (fixed gear)

================================================================================
POWERPLANT LIMITATIONS
================================================================================

Engine Model:       Lycoming O-320-H2AD
Maximum Horsepower: 160 HP at 2700 RPM
Maximum RPM:        2700 RPM
Oil Temperature:    245°F maximum
                     75°F minimum for takeoff
Oil Pressure:       25 psi minimum (at idle)
                     60–90 psi normal operating range
                     115 psi maximum (cold starts only, transient)
Cylinder Head Temp: 500°F maximum
EGT:                No redline; monitor for mixture management

Fuel Grade:         100LL (Aviation Grade 100 Low Lead, colored blue)
                    100 Octane Aviation Gasoline (colored green) also approved
                    NOTE: automotive fuel (mogas) is NOT approved for this aircraft

Oil Grade:          SAE 50 or SAE 40 (above 16°C)
                    SAE 30 or SAE 40 (between 0°C and 16°C)
                    SAE 30 (below 0°C)
                    Multiviscosity 15W-50 aviation oil approved per Lycoming SB 480F
                    Lycoming LW-16702 anti-wear additive required per AD 80-04-03 R2
                    (unless using pre-additized AeroShell W 15W-50 or AeroShell W 100 Plus)

Oil Capacity:       8 quarts maximum
                    6 quarts minimum for flight

Engine TBOH:        2000 hours (Lycoming recommended, not FAA mandatory for non-commercial ops)

================================================================================
WEIGHT LIMITS
================================================================================

Maximum Gross Weight:           2300 lbs
Maximum Ramp Weight:            2308 lbs
Maximum Zero Fuel Weight:       No limit
Baggage Area Weight (aft):      120 lbs maximum
Baggage Area Moment:            See weight and balance section

================================================================================
CENTER OF GRAVITY LIMITS
================================================================================

Forward CG Limit:  35.0 inches aft of datum (at gross weight)
                   36.5 inches aft of datum (at 1950 lbs)
Aft CG Limit:      47.3 inches aft of datum (all weights)
Reference Datum:   Leading edge of firewall

================================================================================
MANEUVER LIMITS
================================================================================

Category:    Normal
             This aircraft is certificated in the Normal category only.
             All aerobatic maneuvers including spins are PROHIBITED.
             Intentional spins are prohibited.

Load Factor: +3.8g to -1.52g (Normal category at gross weight)
             Reduced with flaps extended.

================================================================================
FUEL LIMITS
================================================================================

Total Fuel Capacity:   43 gallons usable (two wing tanks, 21.5 gal each)
                       45 gallons total (1 gal unusable per tank)
Minimum Fuel for IFR:  Per 14 CFR 91.151 and 91.167

Unusable Fuel (each tank): 1 gallon

Approved Fuel Grades: Aviation Grade 100LL (blue)
                      Aviation Grade 100 (green)

================================================================================
FLIGHT CREW
================================================================================

Minimum Crew:   1 pilot
                Dual control capable — co-pilot controls fully functional

================================================================================
KINDS OF OPERATION
================================================================================

Day VFR:       Approved
Night VFR:     Approved (with required equipment per 14 CFR 91.205)
Day IFR:       Approved (with required equipment per 14 CFR 91.205)
Night IFR:     Approved (with required equipment per 14 CFR 91.205)
Icing:         NOT approved for flight into known icing conditions

================================================================================
SYSTEM LIMITATIONS
================================================================================

Vacuum System (gyros):
  Minimum vacuum for gyro operation: 4.5 in. Hg
  Normal vacuum range: 4.5–5.4 in. Hg

Electrical System:
  28-volt DC system
  60-amp alternator
  24-volt battery

Transponder:
  24-month inspection required per 14 CFR 91.413

ELT:
  Battery replacement/inspection required per 14 CFR 91.207
  (12 months from date of last inspection, or after activation, or at 50% battery life)

Pitot-Static System:
  24-month inspection required for IFR flight per 14 CFR 91.411

================================================================================
PLACARDS (REQUIRED IN-COCKPIT)
================================================================================

  "THIS AIRPLANE IS APPROVED FOR THE FOLLOWING OPERATIONS WHEN EQUIPPED
   PER THE APPLICABLE FAR: DAY VFR, NIGHT VFR, DAY IFR, NIGHT IFR"

  "NO ACROBATIC MANEUVERS INCLUDING SPINS APPROVED"

  "FUEL SELECTOR VALVE: LEFT — BOTH — RIGHT — OFF"
  (RIGHT tank fuel must be used with LEFT tank to prevent asymmetric loading)

  "IN EMERGENCY, PULL TO UNLOCK" (door placard)
