FEDERAL AVIATION ADMINISTRATION
AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVE

AD Number:    90-06-03 R1
Amendment:    39-6966 (Revision 1; effective March 27, 1991)

APPLICABILITY: Cessna Aircraft Company Models 172, 172RG, R172K, 182, 182RG,
               T182, R182, 185, 188, 206, T206, 207, 210, T210, and others
               equipped with Continental or Lycoming engine exhaust heater/muffler
               assemblies manufactured by Cessna or approved manufacturers.

               Desert Sky Aviation fleet: N4798E, N2251K, N8834Q, N1156P
               (Cessna 172N, Lycoming O-320-H2AD) — all affected.

EFFECTIVE DATE: March 27, 1991

SUBJECT: Exhaust Heater/Muffler Assembly — Cracks and Carbon Monoxide Intrusion

================================================================================
BACKGROUND
================================================================================

Cessna singles use a "heat muff" or exhaust heat exchanger (muffler/heater
assembly) that routes outside air over the exhaust system to produce cabin heat.
This system relies on the integrity of the exhaust collector and muffler assembly
to prevent combustion gases (including carbon monoxide, CO) from entering the
heated air stream delivered to the cockpit.

In service, Cessna identified cracking in the exhaust heater/muffler assembly
from thermal cycling fatigue. Cracks in the inner walls of the heat exchanger
allow CO to contaminate cabin heat air. CO is colorless, odorless, and highly
toxic; CO poisoning is subtle and has caused fatal accidents in GA aircraft.

Factors accelerating cracking:
  - High-time exhaust systems (typical life 800–1,200 hours)
  - Excessive heat (high CHT/EGT operations)
  - Deferred maintenance on exhaust gaskets (leaks cause hot spots)
  - Operation with cabin heat on high for extended periods

================================================================================
COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

COMPLIANCE INTERVAL:
  Initial: Within 100 hours TIS from effective date, or next annual inspection,
           whichever occurs first.
  Recurring: At each annual inspection, AND at each 100-hour inspection if
             performed, AND any time new exhaust system components are installed.

REQUIRED ACTIONS:

  1. VISUAL INSPECTION — EXTERIOR:
     Inspect all accessible exterior surfaces of the exhaust system including:
       a. Exhaust stacks (risers) at each cylinder for cracks, especially at welds
       b. Exhaust collector ring for cracks, corrosion, and loose joints
       c. Muffler body for cracks, dents, or corrosion
       d. Heat exchanger shroud for cracks and deformation
       e. All clamps, hangers, and baffles for security and condition

  2. INTERNAL INSPECTION — MUFFLER:
     With exhaust inlet plugged (to illuminate interior), visually inspect
     inside the muffler/heat exchanger for cracks in the inner wall separating
     exhaust gases from heated outside air.

     Method: Insert a bright light source into the exhaust outlet; inspect
     for light transmission through inner wall cracks from outside.
     Alternative: Conduct pressure test per Cessna Maintenance Manual.

  3. CRACK CRITERIA:
     Any crack found in:
       a. Exhaust riser welds or walls: REPLACE before further flight
       b. Collector ring: REPLACE before further flight
       c. Muffler inner wall: REPLACE muffler/heat exchanger before further flight
       d. Exterior muffler body: REPAIR or REPLACE before further flight
       (cracks through exterior wall are less critical but must still be addressed)

  4. CARBON MONOXIDE DETECTOR:
     It is strongly recommended (but not required by this AD) to install a
     CO detector in the cockpit. A properly functioning CO detector provides
     early warning if the exhaust system develops a crack between annuals.

================================================================================
ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

If any exhaust component is replaced, the replacement must be:
  - FAA-approved (PMA-approved or manufactured under TC data)
  - Installed per Cessna Maintenance Manual and applicable STC if applicable
  - Leak-tested after installation per Cessna maintenance procedures

Exhaust system component service life:
  Lycoming O-320 exhaust risers and muffler: no hard TBOH, but typically
  800–1,200 hours. Inspect condition annually and replace proactively.

COMPLIANCE DOCUMENTATION

================================================================================
NOTES FOR DESERT SKY AVIATION OPERATORS
================================================================================

  • Each aircraft requires recurring inspection per this AD at annual and 100-hour
    (if applicable), and whenever exhaust components are replaced.
  • Document compliance with AD 90-06-03 R1 in the maintenance log with hobbs/tach.
  • Installation of a cockpit carbon monoxide detector is strongly recommended;
    follow manufacturer replacement intervals.
  • High-time exhaust assemblies warrant careful visual and internal inspection;
    replace components that meet the AD crack criteria before further flight.
